"Dr. Muhammad Yunus, often referred to as "Dr. Yunus," is a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur, economist, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate. He is renowned for founding the Grameen Bank and pioneering the concept of microcredit and microfinance, which provides small loans to impoverished people without requiring collateral. His innovative approach has empowered millions, especially women, to escape poverty by starting small businesses. In 2006, Dr. Yunus and the Grameen Bank jointly received the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts to create economic and social development from below. His work has made a significant impact on global poverty reduction."
